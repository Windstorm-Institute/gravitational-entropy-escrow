## Cover-letter abstract

We propose a physical reframing under which gravity and entropy are revealed as the same phenomenon viewed through different vocabularies. Gravitational binding energy is entropy held in escrow against the local Unruh temperature; forces felt by an observer are gradients of escrowed entropy in that observer's frame. Under the escrow postulate, Newton's law (Verlinde 2011), Bekenstein–Hawking entropy at horizons, the equivalence principle (read as frame-dependence of escrow gradients), and the deep-MOND Tully–Fisher relation with `a_0` set by the de Sitter floor temperature `T_dS = ℏc√(Λ/3)/(2πk_B)`, all follow from one principle. The paper is explicit that the mathematical machinery is due to Bekenstein, Hawking, Unruh, Jacobson, Padmanabhan, Verlinde, and van Putten; the offered contribution is the picture that connects them.

We engage quantitatively with empirical anchors: a five-case extension of Milgrom's (2017) test on the Genzel et al. (2017) high-z galaxies independently disfavors `H(z)`-tracking of `a_0` alongside the `(1+z)^(3/2)` exclusion; a SPARC reanalysis (~140 deep-MOND galaxies) yields global `a_0 ≈ 1.24 × 10⁻¹⁰ m/s²`. The framework's bare Λ-form prediction lies ~25% below the empirical value; the residual is identified as an order-unity coefficient `α ≈ 1.34` (or ≈ 0.21 against the raw, un-2π-suppressed de Sitter scale — see §8.4.3) awaiting derivation from horizon-matched thermodynamics, not absorbed by parameter-fitting. The radial cluster-core mass-discrepancy pattern is flagged as a real difficulty the framework cannot dissolve in its present form. Reproducible Python analysis scripts archived alongside.

## Why Entropy / MDPI

This paper sits squarely at the intersection of (i) thermodynamics-of-gravity / entropic-gravity construction, (ii) cosmological thermodynamics (de Sitter horizon as Carnot floor), and (iii) interpretation of established results (Bekenstein, Hawking, Unruh, Jacobson, Padmanabhan, Verlinde, van Putten). Entropy regularly publishes both technical contributions and synthesis articles in this space. The paper's posture is interpretive rather than derivational, with explicit limitations enumerated and a quantitative empirical test (Genzel five-case) that distinguishes the framework's Λ-form commitment from the H(z)-form variants in the same family.
